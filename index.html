Information Metabolism & The 8 Functions

The foundational elements of the psyche. Rather than behavioral traits, MBTI defines the specific neurological and psychological pathways through which an individual perceives data (Irrational functions) and organizes that data into decisions (Rational functions).

Dynamic Syntax & Relationship of Symbols

To move beyond amateur profiling, we mathematically define the cognitive stack. Functions do not operate in isolation; they exist on polarized axes. Pushing one end of the axis inevitably pulls the other.

The Psychic State Equation

An individual's cognitive orientation (Ψ) is the sum of their functions (F), modified by their attitude (a : e/i) and their hierarchical weight/position (ω).

Ψ(type) = Σ [ ω_n( F_x(a) ) ] + (Environmental Variance)

Where ω_1 (Dominant) = 4, ω_2 (Aux) = 3, ω_3 (Tert) = 2, ω_4 (Inf) = 1. Shadow functions possess negative ω weights (unconscious tension).

Cognitive Quadras & Synchronicity

Types that share the exact same four cognitive functions (regardless of order) belong to a "Quadra". These groups share fundamental philosophical baselines and communicate with zero cognitive translation required, leading to high synergy.

Volume represents shared functional valuation within the Quadra.

Alpha Quadra (INTP, ENTP, ISFJ, ESFJ)

Values: Ti, Fe, Si, Ne. Focuses on theoretical frameworks, pleasant social atmospheres, and intellectual exploration without immediate pragmatic constraints.

Beta Quadra (INFJ, ENFJ, ISTP, ESTP)

Values: Ni, Se, Ti, Fe. Focuses on grand visions, intense physical/emotional experiences, logical tactics, and collective systemic change.

Gamma Quadra (INTJ, ENTJ, ISFP, ESFP)

Values: Ni, Se, Fi, Te. Focuses on long-term strategy, objective utility, deep personal authenticity, and impactful real-world execution.

Delta Quadra (INFP, ENFP, ISTJ, ESTJ)

Values: Si, Ne, Fi, Te. Focuses on personal growth, practical stabilization, improving localized systems, and idiosyncratic potential.

Clinical Pathologies: Loops & Grips

Typology provides a framework for diagnosing specific cognitive distress states. These are not permanent disorders, but mechanical breakdowns in information metabolism.

!

The Inferior Grip (Eruption)

Occurs under immense, sudden stress or chronic exhaustion of the dominant function. The psyche flips, and the undeveloped 4th function takes tyrannical control.

  • IxxJ (Se Grip): Binge eating, reckless spending, obsession with physical details, sensory hyper-reactivity.
  • ExxP (Ni Grip): Paranoia, singular catastrophic visions of the future, conspiracy theories, withdrawal from reality.
  • IxxP (Fe/Te Grip): Sudden tyrannical attempts to control others' logic/behavior (Te), or sudden desperate need for external validation/emotional outbursts (Fe).
  • ExxJ (Fi/Ti Grip): Absolute self-isolation, extreme harsh self-judgment (Fi), or convoluted, illogical rationalizations destroying their own systems (Ti).
∞

The Dominant-Tertiary Loop

A chronic defense mechanism. The individual bypasses their 2nd (Auxiliary) function to avoid dealing with the opposite attitude (e/i), creating an echo chamber.

  • Introverted Loops (e.g., Ni-Ti in INFJ): Detachment from external reality. Constructing massive, intricate internal theories or self-criticisms with zero real-world data collection.
  • Extraverted Loops (e.g., Ne-Fe in ENTP): Complete loss of internal grounding. Desperate rapid-fire idea generation purely to manipulate external social reactions, losing all personal logical consistency.
  • Treatment involves forcefully activating the Auxiliary function to break the loop.
